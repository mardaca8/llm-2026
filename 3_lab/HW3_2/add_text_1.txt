
Simultaneously, the concept of adaptive reuse has gained momentum as a counter-narrative to the "demolish and rebuild" cycle of the past. Architects are increasingly finding value in the "embodied carbon" of existing structures, transforming derelict factories, warehouses, and silos into vibrant modern hubs. This practice honors the historical layers of the urban landscape while significantly reducing the environmental impact associated with new construction. It represents a shift in philosophy where the most sustainable building is often the one that already exists, reimagined for a new century.
