Free healthcare has both positive and negative implications for treatment quality. While changing the inequity in the healthcare system for the better, such modifications can lead to severe problems with the governmental budget distribution and treatment quality. Each country’s government should accurately analyze the free healthcare perspectives and possible drawbacks to develop the individual strategy of improving citizens’ wellbeing. When appropriately planned, free healthcare programs’ advantages outweigh the disadvantages.

The Clockmaker's Daughter

You are a Clockmaker. You have been for as long as you can remember. You have always been fascinated by the intricate workings of timepieces, and you have spent your life perfecting your craft.

But now, the world has changed. The power has gone out, and the clocks have stopped. You are alone in a world without electricity, and you must find a way to keep time moving.

You begin by repairing the clocks in your home. You take them apart, one by one, and you clean the gears and the springs. You oil the mechanisms, and you put them back together again.

You work for hours, and when you are finished, the clocks are running perfectly. You feel a sense of satisfaction, but you know that this is only the beginning.

You go out into the world, and you begin to repair the clocks in the town. You work tirelessly, and you soon become known as the Clockmaker. People come to you from all over, and they bring you their broken clocks.

You repair them all, and you keep the town running on time. You are the only one who can do this, and you take pride in your work.

But one day, you receive a clock that you cannot repair. It is too damaged, and the gears are too worn. You try everything you can think of, but you cannot make it work.

You are disappointed, but you know that this is just one clock. There are still many others that you can repair.

You continue your work, and you keep the town running on time. But you cannot help but think about the clock that you could not repair.

You wonder if there is something that you could have done differently. You wonder if there is a way to fix it.

You work harder than ever, and you repair more clocks than ever before. But you cannot forget about the one that got away.

You work until your hands are raw, and you work until your eyes are red. But you cannot fix the clock that you could not repair.

You are tired, and you are frustrated. You are ready to give up.

But then, you remember the metaphor of the broken hourglass. You remember that time is always moving, even when the clocks are stopped.

You remember that you are the Clockmaker, and you are the only one who can keep time moving.

You take a deep breath, and you begin to work again. You repair the clocks, and you keep the town running on time.

You are the Clockmaker, and you will never give up.
